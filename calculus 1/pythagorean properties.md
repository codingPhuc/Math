$$
\cos(\circ)^2  + \sin (\circ)^2  = 1  
$$


example if $\sin(t) = -\frac{2}{7}$ and  t is angle that lies in quadrant III , find the $\cos(t)$ 

$$
\begin{array}{l} \\
\cos t^2   +  \sin t^2  = 1  \\ 
(\cos t)^2  +\left( -\frac{2}{7} \right)^2  =1   \\
(\cos t)^2  +  \frac{4}{49}   = 1   \\
(\cos t)^2    =  1  - \frac{4}{49}    =  \frac{45}{49}    \\
\cos  t =   \pm  \frac{\sqrt{ 45 }}{7}
\end{array}
$$
