$$
\begin{array}{l}
 \frac{1}{2}  =  \frac{4}{8} \\
1\cdot 8  =  2\cdot 4 
\end{array}{}
$$
Cross multiplication nean that you multiple  the numerator of  the left side with  the denominator of the right side and vice versa 
$$
\begin{array}{l}
 \frac{3}{8}  =  \frac{9}{x} \\
3\cdot x  =  8\cdot 9  \\
x  = \frac{72}{3}
\end{array}{}
$$
EX : 

$$
\begin{array}{l}
\frac{2x + 1}{7}  = \frac{x - 3 }{5} \\
\frac{}{7} (2x + 1)   = \frac{x - 3 }{5} \\
x  = \frac{72}{3}
\end{array}{}
$$
